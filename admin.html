About "Myself

Olúwadámilọ́lá Akínwùnmí

Co-founder, Thinking True Initiative

I am known by the name Olúwadámilọ́lá Akínwùnmí. I am currently based in Abuja, Nigeria, where I work as a civil servant with the FCT Administration (Agriculture Secretariat). I am married to my lovely wife. I obtained my Bachelor’s degree from Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife, under the Faculty of Agriculture, in 2012. Beyond these details, who I am today is the product of the deliberate humbling process by the Father (God). The humble background into which I was born, combined with prolonged academic struggles, drew me into deeper intimacy with God’s pruning. This process became more concrete between 2012 – 2024 where I went through the core Optimality phase of Thinking True (Selfless Faith).

During this period, I faced grave challenges and seasons of loneliness. I wrote two books, pioneered a community focused on self-development, mastered my natural talent in music, and taught teenagers music as part of that mastery. Throughout this time, my feeling faculty remained highly active, allowing experience to be processed with depth rather than suppression. Above all, the teachings of Jesus became lived principles in my life—most notably the discipline of first (optimally) considering the log in one’s own eye.

I am not a product of the systems of this world, but of the Father, who, through my humbled self, intends that many more come into begotten sonship.

WHY GIVE THIS SERIOUS CONSIDERATION?

This era, marked by the advent of Artificial Intelligence, belongs to those who can discover the limits of what society compels them to take seriously—career, morality, achievement, and status—and therefore learn to take themselves seriously from within. It is by encountering these limits that Reasoning begins to look beyond external validation and become inwardly responsible for its own life.

This seriousness is not formed by affirmation, self-talk, or conscious resolve. It emerges as Reasoning undergoes a living, inward formation—continually shaped by the divine will of God—where self-challenge becomes life itself. In this process, one is not a product of world systems, but inwardly begotten of the Father.

(Jeremiah 31:33; Romans 8:19)
