Other Dimensions To Our Methodology

Calender Structure

A session spans 39 weeks (3 cycles).

Each cycle (13 weeks) consists of: 4 weeks of online engagement (once weekly), 1 week mid-cycle break, another 4 weeks of online engagement (once weekly), and 4 weeks of break.

Each ward progresses through 6 sessions (approximately 4.5 years): 3 sessions in the Beginner Tier, 2 sessions in the Intermediary Tier, and 1 session in the Advanced Tier.

Online Classes / Meetings

A weekly meeting lasts 1 hour 20 minutes, scheduled across weekday evenings and weekends.

Meetings alternate between:

Variety Sessions (one macro and one micro activity), and

Core Sessions (one macro activity alongside the core course).

Each type occurs four times per cycle.

Personal Mentorship of the Ward

Because wards are engaged at a level beyond their age, online meetings alone are not enough. Each child therefore receives ongoing personal mentorship in collaboration with parents. This mentorship supports growth in intentionality, self-mastery, time management, and responsibility. It is conducted professionally and always with parental knowledge and cooperation.

Conditions for Fruitful Engagement at the Academy

Enrolment into the Academy of Intelligence – Reality’s Realm presupposes alignment with the nature of the work. Because this Academy is concerned with living, inward growth, the following conditions must be present for a child to pass through it fruitfully.

These are not institutional terms, but conditions of life and formation.

Recognition of the Three Foundational Platforms of the Child’s Development +

By enrolling a child into the Academy, parents affirm that the child’s healthy development rests on three equally important platforms: the religion practiced, formal academics, and this Academy. Enrolment implies that parents are committed to actively supporting the child across all three platforms, without allowing one to undermine the others.

Intentional and Active Parental Collaboration +

This Academy functions by intentional and active collaboration, not substitution. Parents are expected to remain actively involved in the child’s development, reinforce self-mastery and responsibility at home, and give this work seriousness comparable to formal academics. Membership fees support structure but cannot replace parental presence or devotion.

Teachable and Responsive Disposition in the Child +

The Academy is not designed for outward brilliance alone. A child must be open to retaining humility, curiosity, and willingness to be inwardly challenged. Confusion, struggle, and resistance are expected; persistent closedness is not.

Openness to Growth as a Gradual and Uneven Process +

Growth within this Academy is not instant, linear, or performance-based. Parents and children must allow room for confusion, self-challenge, silence, and gradual inward awakening. Premature pressure for visible results disrupts inward formation.

Relational and Communal Engagement +

The Academy involves:

  • gradual relational synergy between the child and the mentor, and,
  • from later sessions, intentional interaction among wards.
These interactions are formative rather than social and contribute to the child’s growth and assessment.

Participatory Assessment Guided by Responsiveness +

The Academy employs a participatory assessment system focused on responsiveness, not competition. Children are not informed of grading metrics or criteria. Assessment remains internal and quiet, serving discernment rather than motivation. Parents are alerted only when responsiveness consistently declines, and such moments are handled through dialogue rather than penalty.

Discernment Rather Than Institutional Validation +

The Academy of Intelligence – Reality’s Realm is not registered under societal or institutional systems. This is not an act of resistance, but an acknowledgment that inward formation cannot be certified, standardized, or regulated by external social frameworks. Participation is therefore based on discernment and resonance, not accreditation.
