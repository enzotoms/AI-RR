W e l c o m e

The Thinking True Initiative has founded the Academy of Intelligence – Reality’s Realm which is purposed at collaborating
with Spiritually Inclined parents to groom the developing Minds of their teenage children (ages 13 – 19) towards Thinking True.
The focus is on children because the developing mind possesses a humility that adulthood often loses. Before identity becomes fixed by ambition, fear, and performance, Reasoning remains malleable and responsive to inward direction.
For this reason, the Academy of Intelligence – Reality’s Realm has been established not to replace education, but to provide a foundation beneath it, ensuring that Reasoning is indeed alive.

Thinking True: Reasoning Being Truly Alive

Thinking True is the spiritual venture initiated by Jesus Christ through His core expositions. Because these expositions are spirit and life, Thinking True is not founded upon cognitive comprehension, doctrinal mastery, or mental assent, but upon experience (spirit) leading to practice (life). Where experience is absent, spirituality remains theoretical.

"It is the spirit that quickeneth; the flesh profiteth nothing: the words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life."

Reasoning becomes truly alive through intimacy with its divine source, as Jesus explicitly revealed:

"The kingdom of God cometh not with observation: Neither shall they say, Lo here! or, lo there! For, behold, the kingdom of God is within you."

This "within you" does not refer to a physical location or organ, nor to a collective possession shared among people. It speaks of intimacy—an inward experience known only by the individual.

Beyond the Placeholder of Religion – God’s Immersive Experience

In many expressions, religion has functioned as a placeholder—offering structure without intimacy, and belief without inward life. Jesus addressed this condition directly:

“Many will say to me… and then will I profess unto them, I never knew you.”

And again: “Not every one that saith unto me, Lord, Lord… but he that doeth the will of my Father.” These words reveal that true spirituality is not measured by religious activity, but by inward conformity to the Father’s will. Jesus further clarified:

“No man can come to me, except the Father which hath sent me draw him.”

Accordingly, the present aim of this Initiative is to orient the child’s developing mind toward vulnerability to God’s inward drawing, as the child (Reasoning) encounters the living virtue of Thinking True as Selfless Faith—faith whose sole drive and sole reward is God.
